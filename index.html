Causes and Effects

Coral is dying, and so is our ocean. Human activity, like the increase of greenhouse gass emissions, is the main cause of climate change. These changes have a broad range of observed effects that are synonymous with the term, such as global warming.

Ocean acidification is the ongoing decrease in the pH of the Earth's oceans, caused by the uptake of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. The main cause of ocean acidification is the burning of fossil fuels. At Coral, our vision is to protect the natural basin, which encompasses 71 percent of our world, while also countering human-caused errors that endanger the ocean's and marine life's health.

Step-by-step Process

Six step process for Coral's mission to succeed

Stage 1

Collect oocytes and sperm samples from coral eggs from our first target location in the Belize Barrier Reef.

Stage 2

Measure the specimen’s data before gene editing to compare reactions and analyze patterns of the two corals.

Stage 3

Edit to withstand bleaching and environmental stressors, such as rising temperature levels and declining pH.

Stage 4

Fertilize specimen and attach nanosensor to gauge the surrounding water quality and temperature.

Stage 5

Collect data from nano-sensors to determine the effects of the gene-edited coral in the ocean and prepare any adjustments to improve the process.

Stage 6

Place device(s) that'll diffuse calcium carbonate into the ecosystems that house shallow water corals. This mechanism will combat ocean acidification that has affect microecosystems.
